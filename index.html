HERDING

All herding breeds share an instinctual ability to control the movement of other animals. These breeds were developed to gather, herd and protect livestock. These breeds are highly intelligent and require lots of exercise.

SPORTING

Breeds in the sporting group are naturally very active and alert and were first developed to work closely with hunters to locate and/or retrieve small game. There are four sub-types of sporting dogs: spaniels, pointers, retrievers and setters.

WORKING

Breeds in the working group are usually intelligent, strong, watchful, alert and quick to learn. These dogs were bred to assist man and excell in jobs such as sled/cart pulling, guarding property and rescues.

TERRIER

Breeds in the terrier group were bred to hunt and kill vermin as well as guard their families' home or barns. These breeds are very energetic, stubborn and feisty and prospective owners should ensure that they have the time to train and social them.
